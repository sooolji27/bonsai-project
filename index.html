About Bonsai

Bonsai is a Japanese art form consisting of growing and shaping small trees in containers. It originated from the ancient Chinese practice of penjing, where rocks and plants were arranged to create landscapes. However, bonsai focuses on a single tree or group of trees of the same species, with a higher level of refinement aesthetically. The term "Bon-sai" is a Japanese term that translates literally to "tree in a pot". This form of art is derived from an ancient Chinese gardening practice, which was later developed under the influence of Zen Buddhism in Japan. The ultimate goal of cultivating a Bonsai is to create a miniature yet realistic representation of nature in the form of a tree. Unlike genetic dwarf trees, any type of tree can be used to cultivate a bonsai.The purpose of bonsai is primarily for the viewer to contemplate, and for the grower to enjoy the pleasant exercise of effort and ingenuity. Unlike other plant cultivation practices, bonsai is not grown for food production or medicine.
